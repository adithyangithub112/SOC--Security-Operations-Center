# Security Operations Center (SOC)

---

## a. Security Operations Center (SOC)

### Definition

A **Security Operations Center (SOC)** is a centralized function responsible for **continuous monitoring, detection, analysis, and response** to cybersecurity threats within an organization.

### Role of SOC

- Monitor security events and alerts 24×7
- Detect malicious or suspicious activities
- Investigate security incidents using logs and security tools
- Respond to and contain incidents
- Protect organizational assets, systems, and data

### Importance of SOC

- Reduces **Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)** and **Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)**
- Prevents escalation of minor incidents into major breaches
- Enhances visibility into the organization’s security posture
- Supports regulatory compliance and auditing requirements

### Objectives of SOC

- Early detection of threats
- Effective incident response and containment
- Minimize operational and business impact
- Continuous improvement of security controls

---

## b. SOC Hierarchy – L1, L2, L3 Roles and Responsibilities

### SOC Level 1 (L1) – Triage Analyst

**Primary responsibility:** Initial alert handling

**Key Responsibilities**

- Monitor SIEM dashboards
- Perform alert triage and validation
- Identify false positives
- Collect initial evidence
- Escalate confirmed incidents

**Key Skills**

- Log analysis
- SOP adherence
- Basic security knowledge

---

### SOC Level 2 (L2) – Incident Responder

**Primary responsibility:** In-depth investigation and response

**Key Responsibilities**

- Analyze escalated alerts
- Perform root cause analysis
- Correlate logs across multiple sources
- Contain and remediate incidents
- Coordinate with IT and security teams

**Key Skills**

- Incident handling
- Threat analysis
- SIEM and IR tools

---

### SOC Level 3 (L3) – Threat Hunter / Expert

**Primary responsibility:** Advanced threat management

**Key Responsibilities**

- Proactive threat hunting
- Malware and forensic analysis
- Develop detection rules and use cases
- Improve SOC processes and playbooks

**Key Skills**

- Advanced cybersecurity expertise
- Scripting and automation
- Research and threat intelligence

---

## c. Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

### Definition

**Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)** are documented, step-by-step instructions that guide SOC analysts on how to respond to specific alerts or incident types.

### Purpose of SOPs

- Ensure consistency in incident handling
- Reduce analyst error
- Speed up investigation and response
- Assist junior analysts in decision-making
- Maintain compliance and audit readiness

### Example

**Phishing SOP**

1. Analyze email headers
2. Inspect URLs and attachments
3. Check threat intelligence sources
4. Block sender and malicious indicators
5. Reset compromised credentials
6. Update and close ticket

---

## d. Incident Response Plan (IRP)

### Overview

An **Incident Response Plan (IRP)** defines the structured approach an organization follows to **identify, manage, and recover from security incidents**.

### Incident Response Lifecycle

1. Preparation
2. Detection and Analysis
3. Containment
4. Eradication
5. Recovery
6. Lessons Learned

### Importance in SOC Operations

- Enables quick and organized response
- Reduces damage and downtime
- Clearly defines roles and escalation paths
- Prevents confusion during active incidents

---

## e. Ticketing System

### Definition

A **Ticketing System** is used to **track, document, manage, and resolve security alerts and incidents** throughout their lifecycle.

### Ticket Workflow

1. Alert generation
2. Ticket creation
3. Assignment to analyst
4. Investigation and documentation
5. Resolution or escalation
6. Ticket closure

### Common Tools

- **ServiceNow**
- **Jira**
- **TheHive**

### Importance

- Maintains accountability
- Provides audit trails
- Enables performance tracking
- Supports knowledge sharing

---

## f. Incident Workflows

### SOC Incident Workflow

1. **Identification**
    - Alert generated by SIEM or security tools
2. **Triage**
    - Validate alert and assess severity
3. **Investigation**
    - Analyze logs, indicators, systems, and scope
4. **Containment**
    - Isolate affected systems
    - Block malicious IPs/domains
    - Disable compromised accounts
5. **Eradication**
    - Remove malware
    - Patch vulnerabilities
6. **Recovery**
    - Restore services
    - Monitor systems for recurrence
7. **Closure and Lessons Learned**
    - Close incident ticket
    - Update SOPs and detection rules
